How OncoScan Works

Three steps from image upload to diagnosis — powered by a VGG16 deep learning model trained on real breast ultrasound data.

01

Upload Ultrasound Image

Drag and drop or browse to upload your breast ultrasound image in JPG or PNG format. No account needed.

02

AI Analyses the Image

The image is preprocessed — resized to 300×300px, normalised — and passed through our trained VGG16 model for deep feature extraction.

03

Get Instant Results

View the classification — Benign, Malignant, or Normal — along with confidence scores and class probabilities for full transparency.

Under the Hood

Three Models, One Winner

We trained and compared MLP, VGG16, and ResNet50. VGG16 delivered the best performance on breast ultrasound classification.

Baseline

MLP

A multi-layer perceptron with 6 dense layers. Fast to train but lacks spatial feature extraction, limiting accuracy on image data.

~70%

Typical Accuracy

⭐ Best Model

VGG16

A 16-layer CNN pre-trained on ImageNet. Transfer learning gives it powerful feature extraction with excellent accuracy on medical imaging.

~90%

Typical Accuracy

Challenger

ResNet50

A 50-layer deep residual network. Strong performance with skip connections but heavier to run than VGG16 for this dataset size.

~85%
